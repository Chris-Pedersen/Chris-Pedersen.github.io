Welcome to my web page! I am a PhD student in the Cosmoparticle Initiative at UCL supervised by Andreu Font-Ribera and Tom Kitching. My research area is cosmology, which is the field of physics concerned with studying the universe on the largest length and time scales, attemping to answer fundamental questions such as what is the universe made of, how old is it, and how can we describe its evolution.

Research Summary

I completed my undergraduate and masters degrees at Cardiff University, where my thesis work was in the LIGO collaboration working on the parameter estimation and degeneracies in the waveforms of binary black hole mergers. The focus of my current research is on the hydrodynamical simulations that are used to generate theoretical realisations of the growth of structure in different cosmological models. In particular, I am working on the application of these codes to the modelling of the Lyman alpha forest, a series of absorption lines in the spectra of distant quasars. The clustering of these absorption lines is closely related to the underlying dark matter density field, and so a careful study of their statistics can be used to constrain cosmology provided both theoretical and observational systematics are well understood. I am also interested in neutrino cosmology, cross correlations of cosmological probes, and statistical techniques in data analysis.
