Welcome to my web page! I am a PhD student in the Cosmoparticle Initiative at UCL. My research area is cosmology, which is the field of physics concerned with studying the universe on the largest length and time scales, attemping to answer fundamental questions such as what is the universe made of, how old is it, and how can we describe its evolution. In particular, I am interested in the intersection between cosmology and particle physics, and the role of massive neutrinos in the growth of structure in the universe. On this site you will find some pedagogical pages on some exciting areas of current research and visualisations of the cosmology simulations that we run as part of our research programme, as well as other bits and bobs.
